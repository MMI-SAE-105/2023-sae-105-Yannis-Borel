SOCIAL REALISM

Social realism is the term used for work produced by painters, printmakers, photographers, writers, and filmmakers that aims to draw attention to the actual socio-political conditions of the working class as a means of criticizing the power structures behind those conditions. Although the characteristics of the movement vary from nation to nation, it almost always employs a form of descriptive or critical realism.
